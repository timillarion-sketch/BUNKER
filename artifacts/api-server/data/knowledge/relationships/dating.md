# Knowledge Base: Dating Relationships

## Overview
Dating relationships involve two people engaging in social and romantic activities together with the intention of assessing each other's suitability as a partner in an intimate relationship or marriage. This knowledge base covers key concepts, common challenges, and evidence-based approaches.

## Attachment Theory
Attachment theory, developed by John Bowlby and Mary Ainsworth, describes how humans form emotional bonds. In adult romantic relationships, four attachment styles are recognized:

- **Secure**: Comfortable with intimacy and independence. Trusting, confident in relationships.
- **Anxious-Preoccupied**: Craves closeness, fears abandonment, often seeks reassurance.
- **Dismissive-Avoidant**: Values independence over intimacy, uncomfortable with closeness.
- **Fearful-Avoidant (Disorganized)**: Desires closeness but fears it simultaneously. Mixed signals.

## Communication Patterns

### Effective Communication
- Use "I" statements to express feelings without blame
- Practice active listening: reflect back what you heard
- Validate your partner's emotions even if you disagree
- Choose the right time and place for important conversations

### Common Communication Pitfalls
- Stonewalling (withdrawal from interaction)
- Criticism (attacking character instead of behavior)
- Defensiveness (denying responsibility)
- Contempt (disrespect, mockery, hostility)

## Conflict Resolution
The Gottman Institute research shows that 69% of relationship conflicts are unsolvable perpetual problems. The goal is not to eliminate conflict but to manage it constructively:

1. Start conversations gently (soft startup)
2. Accept influence from your partner
3. Make repair attempts during heated moments
4. Compromise where possible
5. Physiologically soothe yourself and your partner

## Relationship Stages
1. **Attraction/Limerence** (3-36 months): Intense romantic feelings, idealization
2. **Power Struggle**: Differences emerge, boundaries are tested
3. **Stability/Commitment**: Acceptance of differences, deeper partnership
4. **Conscious Partnership**: Intentional growth together
5. **Transformation**: Long-term shared meaning and purpose

## Red Flags
- Controlling behavior (monitoring whereabouts, finances)
- Verbal or physical aggression
- Gaslighting (denying reality, making partner doubt themselves)
- Love bombing (excessive flattery early on)
- Lack of accountability
- Boundary violations

## Healthy Relationship Indicators
- Mutual respect and trust
- Effective conflict resolution
- Shared values and goals
- Emotional and physical safety
- Individual autonomy within partnership
- Consistent, reliable behavior
